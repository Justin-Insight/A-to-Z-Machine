    values:
      - heading: What does MIG stand for?
        body: MIG stands for Metal Inert Gas welding. It is also called Gas Metal Arc Welding (GMAW). The process uses a continuously fed wire electrode and shielding gas, typically argon or CO2, to weld metals quickly and efficiently, especially on thicker materials. 
      - heading: What is MIG welding used for?
        body: MIG welding is used for welding thick materials, especially steel and aluminum, in automotive, construction and manufacturing industries. It offers high speed, ease of use and strong welds.
      - heading: What are the advantages of MIG welding?
        body: The advantages of MIG welding include fast welding speed and strong welds on thick metals. It supports continuous wire feeding for efficiency and reduces downtime. MIG welding is versatile, suitable for various metals and commonly used in automotive and industrial fabrication.
      - heading: What materials can be welded with MIG? 
        body: MIG welding can be used on steel, stainless steel, aluminum, copper, nickel and magnesium alloys. It works best on thick materials and is favored for its versatility across both ferrous and non-ferrous metals.
      - heading: What is the difference between MIG vs TIG welding?
        body: The main difference between MIG and TIG welding is in technique and application. MIG welding uses a consumable wire for fast, easy welds on thicker materials. TIG welding uses a non-consumable tungsten electrode for precise, clean welds on thinner or specialty metals. MIG suits speed; TIG suits precision.
